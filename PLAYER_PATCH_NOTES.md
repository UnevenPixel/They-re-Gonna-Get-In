# They're Gonna Get In! — Patch Notes

## Update — July 4, 2026 (in progress)

**Five new recruits have joined the roster**

- Bomb Goblins, Mud Golems, Soldiers, Archers, and Knights are all trainable now, each from its own new building (the Boom Hut, Bog Foundry, Barracks, Archery Range, and Round Table). Every unit brings something different to the field, so it's worth mixing and matching.

**Combat finally has stakes**

- Units and buildings can now actually take damage and be destroyed. Fights matter in a way they didn't before.

**Archers fight at range**

- Archers now fire real arrows at approaching enemies instead of needing to walk up and swing like everyone else.

**Smarter fighting instincts**

- Both your troops and the enemy's now think a bit before picking a target — favoring wounded enemies worth finishing off, bigger threats worth focusing, and whoever's closest — rather than just grabbing whatever's nearest.

**Behind the scenes**

- A round of stability fixes to make sure this update's new content is properly wired in, plus early groundwork for a future progression system.

## Update — July 3, 2026 (in progress)

**Building outside the walls is here**

- You can now drag buildings from your blueprint tray straight onto open plots outside your castle. You'll start with a few Wheat Fields and a Peasant Ward already unlocked so you can try it out right away.

**Wheat Fields put food on the table**

- Plant a Wheat Field and it'll produce wheat for you automatically over time — one more resource to keep an eye on as your base grows.

**Recruit your first troops**

- The Peasant Ward lets you queue up new peasants to help defend your base. Each Ward can only support so many trained units at a time, and your whole army has an overall size limit too, so growing your forces takes some planning.

**Smoother scouting**

- The camera now pans on its own when you move your mouse to the edge of the screen, making it easier to keep track of a bigger battlefield.

**Behind the scenes**

- A round of stability fixes to keep both sides' resources, and unit combat animations, behaving the way they're supposed to.

## Update — July 1, 2026

**Fixes**

- Fixed an issue where selecting your units and right-clicking to give them an order could fail to bring up the command menu.

**The enemy is starting to fight back**

- We've begun testing an opponent that plays by the same rules you do — no cheating, no secret advantages. It watches the battlefield, masses up its troops, and pushes an assault once it's ready. Still early days for it — think "learning the ropes," not "criminal mastermind" — but it's the first real step toward a castle under actual siege from someone other than you.

**Coming soon: building outside the walls**

- We're laying the groundwork for base building beyond your castle's inner grounds. Soon you'll be able to put buildings out in the open field, not just behind your walls. Building out in the open comes with real risk — those spots sit closer to the fighting — but the payoff is worth it for those willing to take the gamble. More on this as it comes together.
